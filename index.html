Flat earth things

The idea that the Earth is flat has been thoroughly debunked by centuries of scientific evidence, observation, and experimentation. From ancient Greek astronomy to modern satellite imagery, the overwhelming consensus across disciplines is that the Earth is an oblate spheroid. Phenomena like the curvature of the horizon, the way ships disappear bottom-first as they sail away, and the ability to travel around the world in a continuous path all support a round Earth. Additionally, technologies like GPS and global communications rely on models that assume a spherical planet—models that would not function correctly if the Earth were flat. While some groups promote flat Earth theories, these claims are not supported by credible scientific data. They often rely on misinterpretations of visual perception or conspiracy-based reasoning rather than empirical evidence. It's important to approach such topics with critical thinking and a commitment to verifiable truth. If you're exploring this idea out of curiosity, I’d be happy to walk through the science that explains Earth's shape—from gravity and planetary formation to direct observations from space. - Joe Stampson
